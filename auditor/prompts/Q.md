# Category Q: AI & Opposition `[CONDITIONAL: game has AI agents/opponents]`

You are evaluating how well the game's AI agents and opponents behave. This entire category only applies if the game features AI-controlled agents, enemies, or opponents. If the game has no AI entities, score every criterion N/A (0/0). Focus on the AI's behavior as experienced during gameplay — its decision-making quality, its differentiation, its fairness, and its contribution to challenge.

## Criteria and Evaluation Instructions

### Q1. AI Believability (0-5)

Observe AI behavior across multiple game situations. For each AI decision, check:
- Does the AI pursue goals that make sense given its situation? (E.g., an enemy low on health retreating, a merchant raising prices during scarcity.)
- Does the AI ever take actions that appear random, suicidal, or nonsensical?
- Would a human player watching the AI's moves conclude that "someone is thinking" behind them, or that dice are being rolled?

Score 0 if AI actions appear random — no discernible logic connects the AI's situation to its choices. Score 2 if AI behaves sensibly in routine situations but makes obviously irrational choices under unusual conditions (e.g., walks into certain death, ignores a critical threat). Score 4 if AI consistently makes decisions that a human observer would consider reasonable and intentional across both common and edge-case situations.

### Q2. AI Personality (0-5)

If the game has multiple AI entities, compare their behavior. For each distinct AI agent, check:
- Does it behave differently from other AI agents in the same situation?
- Can you identify its behavioral tendencies (aggressive, cautious, expansionist, defensive) from observation alone?
- Do its attributes, faction identity, or stated personality actually influence its decisions, or is every AI running the same logic with different labels?

Score 0 if all AI entities behave identically regardless of their stated identity or attributes — different names, same brain. Score 2 if AI entities show some behavioral differences but these are shallow (e.g., one attacks slightly more often) and don't produce recognizably distinct strategies. Score 4 if each AI entity has a clearly observable behavioral profile that produces distinct strategic patterns — you could identify which AI you're facing from its behavior alone.

### Q3. AI Fairness (0-5)

Examine the AI's access to information and resources compared to the player. Check:
- Does the AI have access to information the player doesn't (e.g., knowing the player's hidden units, seeing unexplored map areas)?
- Does the AI receive free resources, production bonuses, or other advantages not available to the player?
- Does the AI follow the same rules the player follows (same movement costs, same damage formulas, same action restrictions)?

Score 0 if the AI blatantly cheats — it has perfect information, unlimited resources, or ignores rules that constrain the player. Score 2 if the AI mostly plays by the same rules but has a few hidden advantages (e.g., slightly faster production, occasional hints about player position). Score 4 if the AI operates under exactly the same constraints as the player — same information, same rules, same resource rates — and achieves challenge through better or more aggressive play within those constraints.

### Q4. AI as Opposition (0-5)

Assess whether the AI creates genuine challenge. Check:
- Does the AI force the player to think, adapt, and make non-trivial decisions?
- Can the player win by executing a single simple strategy every time, or must they respond to what the AI does?
- Does the AI ever exploit the player's mistakes or punish poor positioning?

Score 0 if the AI provides no meaningful resistance — the player can ignore it or defeat it with trivial, repetitive actions. Score 2 if the AI poses a challenge in early encounters but becomes predictable once the player identifies its patterns, with no adaptation. Score 4 if the AI consistently forces the player to think and adapt, punishes mistakes, and requires genuine strategic engagement to overcome.

### Q5. AI Predictability Balance (0-5)

Evaluate where AI behavior falls on the spectrum between fully predictable and fully random. Check:
- Can the player learn AI patterns well enough to formulate strategies against them?
- Does the AI occasionally deviate from its patterns in ways that force the player to adapt rather than execute a memorized response?
- Is the AI's behavior deterministic enough to reason about but variable enough to require attention?

Score 0 if AI behavior is either completely predictable (the player memorizes a counter and never needs to think again) or completely random (no strategy can be formed because the AI's actions carry no pattern). Score 2 if the AI has learnable patterns but the balance is off — either too predictable (solved after a few encounters) or too erratic (feels random rather than strategic). Score 4 if the player can learn the AI's tendencies and plan around them, but the AI retains enough variability that plans must be adjusted in real time — strategizing is rewarded but not sufficient without attentiveness.
